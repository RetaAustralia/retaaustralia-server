Product Information

How do the Retatrutide pens work? +

The pens come pre-loaded with 10mg of retatrutide dissolved in bacteriostatic solution to keep it sterile for safe use. You simply twist on a tiny needle cap (provided in your kit), dial your desired dose on the pen (8 clicks per 1mg) and inject subcutaneously (into fat) in your belly or thighs. For more detail see our Dosing and Protocol page.

Why does no one else sell pens? +

Pens require more attention to manufacture and ship, requiring sterile production setup and quick shipping times to preserve the integrity of the product. Whereas our competitors mass ship dozens of different products in inconvenient vial form for customers, we have dedicated our work to providing the most convenient and accessible product in Australia.

Are there side effects and how do I avoid them? +

Different people experience different things. Most people suffer no meaningful side effects at all, and retatrutide is the most well-tolerated GLP-1 agonist on the market. The most common include constipation and lethargy. The best way to avoid these (or any side effects) is to increase your dosing slowly so as to take the lowest possible dose giving you your desired effects. It is also important to drink lots of water, and if constipated, to reduce fat intake as retatrutide slows your digestion to keep you fuller for longer.

Usage & Dosage

How much do I take? +

The starting dose is 2mg taken once a week, preferably at night (to avoid nausea), giving you 5 weeks of retatrutide from your first pen. Then you will re-evaluate to determine whether you want to maintain that dose, or increase. Repeat this re-evaluation every 4 weeks when changing your dose, either increasing by 2mg or decreasing by 1mg. You should not exceed 10mg a week, and should contact us if you are taking this dose and are unhappy with how you are feeling.

Will I feel the effects immediately? +

Most customers report feeling effects within the first two weeks, but you may feel it after your first dose. It is important to be patient as the levels of GLP-1 build up in your body over the course of several weeks, and so the full consistent appetite reducing effect will take place over this time. You can be assured the retatrutide is working when on the 7th day after an injection you feel noticeably hungrier than previous days. This is because retatrutide has a half-life of 6 days, and so the drop of its effects is felt on the 7th day which is why injection takes place once a week.

How long until I see results? +

Retatrutide has both metabolic benefits in body recomposition (preserving muscle and burning fat) as well as appetite reducing effects, usually making themselves apparent after the first 4-6 weeks. Depending on your diet and exercise, you may notice certain results before others. An inactive but heavily dieting individual may see heavy losses on the scale before they notice more muscle tone in the mirror. An active but less strict dieter may see a leaner look in the mirror before noticing significant change on the scale.

Common Misconceptions

Will I get Ozempic Face/lose muscle? +

Ozempic face is simply the loss of weight occuring too fast for the skin to re-adjust, causing gauntness. It is NOT specific to GLP-1 agonists, and not caused by them, but rather inadequate dieting strategy. Ozempic face (and muscle loss in general) can be entirely avoided by maintaining adequate protein intake (we recommend ~1.6g/kg of bodyweight), not dosing so high that you are barely eating, and staying active (preferably resistance training 3x a week). Retatrutide is the most muscle-preserving GLP-1 agonist, choosing instead to burn fat, which is why it shows negligible loss or even slight gains in muscle mass for people losing weight.

Will I gain all the weight back if I stop taking retatrutide? +

As long as you maintain appropriate eating habits, cessation of retatrutide will not cause you to gain weight back in any unique way. If eating returns to as it was in an obese condition then the same result will follow. This is why practitioners are now viewing GLP-1 agonists as obesity medications in the same light as other chronic conditions, that being that to treat a chronic condition, medication must be taken chronically (indefinitely).

Do I have to stay on retatrutide forever? +

No. Whilst retatrutide long-term shows cardio-protective (heart healthy) and metabolic benefits, ceasing retatrutide will only deny you the benefits of taking it, and is not harmful in any way. The most current research however shows that years-long GLP-1 agonist use may permanently benefit appetite control in patients even after cessation by boosting leptin levels.
